What is this?

This tool helps the LES Ecology Center's tree stewardship program prioritize which street trees need care. It covers the Community Board 3 area — the best geographic proxy for where LES Ecology Center works — mapping all ~11,000 active street trees in the CB3 area from the NYC Forestry live dataset, and ranks every tree by care urgency using a simple two-factor system.

Who is it for?

LES Ecology Center street tree care staff — to plan watering and care walks, identify where young trees are concentrated, and focus effort where it matters most.

How to use it

🗺
Map & Filters
View all active street trees in the CB3 area. Dots are colored by priority tier (red = highest need, green = lowest). Use filters to show only specific categories: Care Needed, Reserved, Tree Guards, Community Stewarded, or Missing/Removed. Click "Clear All" to reset filters.
🌳
Tree Actions
Tap any tree to open details. You can: 📅 Reserve for Care, 🔧 Mark as Needs Care, 🛡 Mark Tree Guard, 🌱 Mark as Community Stewarded, or ⚠️ Report Missing. Each action stores your data and appears in the corresponding filter.
📊
Stats
View summary statistics for the current filter: health breakdown, average priority tier, and the top 10 most common species.

Priority tiers explained

Each tree is assigned one of 4 tiers based on two yes/no questions:

Tier 4 — Young · commercial corridor
Tier 3 — Young · high foot-traffic area
Tier 2 — Mature · commercial corridor
Tier 1 — Mature · high foot-traffic area

A tree is "young" if its trunk diameter is under 6 inches, or if it was planted within the last 5 years. Young trees need the most attention in their first years to establish healthy root systems.

Data sources

NYC Forestry Tree Points (live) — NYC Parks & Recreation / NYC Open Data. The operational forestry database, updated continuously, providing species, health, trunk diameter, planting date, and location for all active street trees in the CB3 area.

Note: Forestry data is updated weekly. Tree conditions may differ from what you find on the ground. This map is a planning aid, not a definitive record.
